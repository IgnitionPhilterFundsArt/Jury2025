16 - Rubatosis

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

Rubatosis is a word created by John Koenig as part of his Dictionary of Obscure Sorrows. The entry for the word defines it as follows: “Rubatosis n. the unsettling awareness of your own heartbeat, whose tenuous muscular throbbing feels less like a metronome than a nervous ditty your heart is tapping to itself, as if to casually remind the outside world, I’m here, I’m here, I’m here.” What makes us feel more alive than being at a burn? The art, the community, the atmosphere; The Pecan Playa is an intoxicating environment that invites our hearts to beat stronger and faster. "Rubatosis" seeks to amplify the heartbeat of participants in the form of fiery bursts that not only mimic their actual pulse, but embody their fiery passion for art. During the day participants will be encouraged to write about what stirs their hearts and ignites their passion, all over the 3.5ft X 5.4 ft metal canvas, painted to resemble the Texas flag. The canvas will sit on a metal easel. The star on the flag will be elevated off the canvas surface, as will a shape of Texas. Further elevated off of the shape of Texas will be a heart. The heart will be covered with Kevlar to act as a wick. Fuel and salts applied to the Kevlar will cause it to burn continuously in multicolor flames when in operation A control box will allow participants to place a finger on a pulse sensor. The sensor will dictate the speed of the two solenoid valves that actuate the flame effect, producing a flame in the same rhythm and speed as the participant’s pulse. Further buttons on the control box may be used to produce flames, lights or smoke.

Have you completed any other large scale art projects? Tell us about it/them: (In this media? Where and when? How did the project go? Did you learn any lessons from those projects we should know?)

I created a large-scale fire effect for Flipside 2025 called "Ring in the Year of the Dragon." The piece was 20ft tall, incorporated LED animations, sound effects, an interactive game and prizes. Making that piece taught me many new skills and valuable lessons. I collaborated on coding, learned to weld, pushed my electronics know how to it's limits, and engaged in glorious battle on the playa with Murphy's Law. At other burns I have created several large art pieces and characters to interact with them. I have run a full-scale apothocary with over 60 potion bottles and elaborate decor, where we mixed drinks and told fortunes. I have used a gas powered sniper rifle to launch marshmellows for burners to catch. I have made large elaborate signs for our camp.
