Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

Elementis: Elementis is an art project that brings the ancient symbols of the classical elements—Air, Fire, Water, Earth, and Spirit (Ether)—into a modern, tangible form. Comprising five aluminum sculptures, each piece represents one of these elements, not just through their ancient symbol representations, but by physically embodying the elements themselves within the negative spaces of the sculptures. Crafted from aluminum, a material chosen for its reflective qualities, its lightweight, and durability, each sculpture features a precisely cut geometric symbol. These symbols are: 1. Air - Upward Triangle with a Line Through It 2. Fire - Upward Triangle 3. Water - Downward Triangle 4. Earth - Downward Triangle with a Line Through It 5. Spirit (Ether - Circle with a Wheel Design The unique aspect of this project lies in how each element is physically integrated into the artwork. The negative spaces within the aluminum structures are not empty voids but are instead integrate the actual elements they represent. This transforms the sculptures from static representations into living, dynamic pieces that interact with their environment and the viewer. - Air: The Air symbol captures the invisible essence of air by allowing natural airflow through its structure. The negative space is designed to channel and reveal the movement of air, revealed by a single feather that flutters in the breeze, making the element's presence felt in a subtle yet powerful way. - Fire: The Fire symbol houses a real flame within its negative space. This propane flame brings the element of fire to life, radiating warmth and energy. The flame not only represents fire’s transformative power but also creates a captivating visual interplay with the aluminum. - Water: The Water symbol features a flowing water element; A dripping fountain will be integrated into its negative space. This element brings the symbol’s fluidity to life, with the water's movement and reflection echoing the changing, reflective nature of this element. - Earth: The Earth symbol is grounded by containing in its negative space a large crystal. This inclusion emphasizes the solidity and nurturing qualities of earth, making the symbol not just a representation but a physical manifestation of stability and grounding. - Spirit (Ether): The Spirit symbol, representing the unifying element, incorporates light and fog into its negative space, symbolizing the ethereal and all-encompassing nature of Spirit as it flows outward and interacts with the other elements. The sculptures are arranged in a circular formation or a large upright star shape, symbolizing the interconnectedness and balance of the elements. The central or top piece, representing Spirit (Ether), serves as the anchor, with the other four elements positioned around it and with it. This arrangement invites viewers to engage with each element individually while also experiencing the harmony and interaction between them. The Elementis project offers a multisensory experience, blending the visual impact of aluminum with the tangible presence of the elements themselves. It invites contemplation on the forces that shape both the natural world and our inner lives, bridging ancient symbolism with contemporary art to create an immersive, dynamic installation. This project not only makes the abstract concepts of the elements accessible but also allows viewers to connect with them in a direct and meaningful way, transforming the unseen forces of nature into a living, breathing experience.
