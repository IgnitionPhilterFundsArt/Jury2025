Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

In the spirit of community art I want to setup a floor loom and encourage everybody to contribute to a set of free form communal tapestries. A table of various art yarns along with non-traditional inclusions such as feathers, flower, etc. will be setup adjacent to it along with instructions. While there will be one if not many finished pieces, this project is primarily about the process and act of collaborative creation. I feel this aligns strongly with the burner philosophy and is indirectly related to this year's theme as the diverse people are what I value most in this state. I find the process to be incredibly relaxing and meditative so it would fit best in a low sound area (or potentially near Sanctuary) there is little someone can do wrong and even mistakes are meant to be embraced as they add to the free form nature of this art style so this is a particularly inclusive interactive piece. The loom will be marked for easy use regardless of the participants' state of mind and body. While there is the bottle neck of only one participant, it can be quite enjoyable to simply watch as well. Special care would be taken to prevent MOOP with precautions such as sealed Tupperware and bags for storage, signage, a covered structure, and a tarp. Once finished the tapestries would be taken off and hung to either be displayed in a community space and brought to future burns in some form, or alternatively burned with the effigy. First and foremost the grant would be used for a variety of yarn and materials to ensure there is enough to encourage creative expression and last the entire burn. the grant would would also go towards creating signage to encourage participation and provide instructions. The loom is the biggest expense currently. I just got one specifically intended for burns, but it is still being paid off on a payment plan. It is an aluminum frame Saori loom that should survive the weather and has several accessibility features so it is more inclusive. While not strictly necessary a larger grant would help pay off the debt on it and provide for repairs should any damage befall it. Should it go well, I hope to expand it and offer more forms of fiber art people can help create next year.
