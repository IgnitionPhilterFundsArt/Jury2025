32 - Pieces of Joy

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

Over the years of burns, I became sick of seeing cars just in camps with no sort of artifying. I decided I wanted to create art to gift (or loan if people don't want to keep it) to folks to use on their cars. The specific art is small quilted panels. These can be painter's taped to windshields or magneted to cars. The smaller pieces are great sizes to cover up adverts on portos to decommodify them to further spread the joy. They're interactive as folks look at how movement happens with the fabric (shape and color of pieces). Some fabrics are silly, some are colorful, some it's just how visual tricks can happen when shapes and fabrics are put back together. It's also interactive for folks who come out to help create pre-event! I would like to teach folks basic quilt piecing at Lloyd and have sewing meet-ups on build days (schedule TBD) so folks can contribute too. The cool thing about quilting is people of any abilities can help as there are many steps in the process: choosing fabrics; fabric cutting; fabric piecing (to make the patterns); sewing; and ironing. Fabrics to be used are ones I have on hand, upcycled fabrics, and donations. As far as grant specific items, in order to improve the efficiency and quality of my work, and make it feasible to work with others at Lloyd, I need more tools to use. Small pieces have been to FZB; more are being made for Flipside.
