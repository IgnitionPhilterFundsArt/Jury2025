6 - Club Nebula

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

Club Nebula redefines the boundaries of immersive art and nightlife. In the heart of a 30x30-foot club, surrounded on all sides by thin black netting, there are three aluminum cubes—standing 7, 8, and 9 feet tall. Lined in LED lights, the cubes pulse and glow in perfect harmony with a fleet of 100 drones soaring directly overhead. This experience invites attendees to step into a surreal, futuristic playground where art comes alive above and around them. Club Nebula redefines how we experience drone artwork, turning the sky into an immersive performance space and the club into a transcendent dreamscape. Flipizens will have three exciting ways to engage with this art installation: -Dance Floor Immersion Step onto the dance floor and become fully immersed in the experience, surrounded by a dazzling array of drones flying overhead. Enclosed by protective netting, the club provides a safe and electrifying space where music, lights, and motion converge into an unforgettable sensory adventure. -Perimeter Viewing For those outside the club, the 360-degree drone show remains a spectacular sight. Whether you're passing by or the club has reached maximum capacity, you can still enjoy the mesmerizing light displays and vibrant energy from the perimeter. The music, dancing, and artistry extend beyond the boundaries, making the experience accessible to everyone. -Hands-On Volunteering Join the magic behind the scenes by volunteering! From assisting with pre-flight checks to managing the show perimeter (front row seat!), this hands-on role offers a unique opportunity to connect with the technology and contribute to bringing the art to life.

In Progress (Making Changes)

What is your timeline detailing the planning, creation, installment, and leave no trace removal of your project?

This will mark the second iteration of Club Nebula, an evolving project that builds on its existing foundation while introducing exciting updates, fresh drone designs, and upgraded equipment. In January, we’ll conduct a comprehensive inventory of all gear and equipment to identify what needs repair or replacement. February will focus on implementing these updates, acquiring new gear, and fine-tuning the technology. By March, we’ll shift our efforts to designing and programming the shows, ensuring a truly unforgettable experience. Our goal is to install the setup as early as possible during the event (timelines may vary as this will be our first time working with early entry logistics). Once the event concludes, all gear will be carefully packed, loaded, and transported offsite. We’re committed to a thorough MOOP sweep of the area, leaving nothing behind and ensuring that the land is not only restored but left better than we found it. This attention to detail reflects our dedication to both the art and the environment.

Have you completed any other large scale art projects? Tell us about it/them: (In this media? Where and when? How did the project go? Did you learn any lessons from those projects we should know?)

Our collective has brought large scale drone projects to Burning Man since 2021 and regional burns since 2023. We brought the first version of Club Nebula to Burning Man in 2023. We had great success and a few magical nights with our club before the rain came and shut down the burn. Since then we’ve been wanting to bring back and up level the club. We learned a lot from our first efforts with Club Nebula including how and where not to route wiring, what to use and not use when fastening our LEDs to the cubes, and most importantly, people will climb on everything even if your art isn’t climbable. *Iph note: There was a video link in the notes but a. it was identifying b, the link didn’t work.
