Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

The past two years, I've had the privilege of bringing a large wooden sculpture to Flipside. This sculpture is titled "Channeled" and was carved from a single piece of cedar using a chainsaw and angle grinder with flap disks. Channeled debuted FS 2023 and was placed on the effigy field. In 2024, Channeled made her home on art island. I would love to bring a new sculpture to Flipside 2025, also carved from a large cedar stump. My style is abstract, largely determined by feeling and flowing with my material. I'm not sure exactly what my new sculpture will look like and therefore don't yet have a name for it, but I imagine it will have a similar vibe as Channeled (though hopefully with more exploration with negative space and shapes) As with Channeled, my hope is that the sculpture can be something that encourages people to take a few moments to touch and feel the sculpture, maybe have a seat next to and get lost in the flow. I also love to make sure the cedar sculpture is lit up at night with color-changing effects to enhance and create new shapes that give the viewer a different feel than daylight viewing. Please note, since this is an organic TBD kind of art, I don't have any renderings of what it will look like. But for images, I've included pics of Channeled (day + night w/ lights) and another, smaller oak sculpture of mine to give ya'll a feel for my style. I've also included photos of the new, raw cedar stump that is waiting for me to get cracking.
