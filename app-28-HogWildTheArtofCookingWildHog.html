28 - Hog Wild, The Art of Cooking Wild Hog

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

My other half and I have brought Dhaba Tacos to Flipside since 2018. It has grown each year and turned into an interactive food experience. Food is a vital part of every person's life but so many people are disconnected from their food and the people who prepare it. At the Dhaba we strive to connect people to each other and their food through a shared culinary experience. In the past we taught people how to make naan and some of the cultural history behind the food that goes with the naan. This year we will keep the crowd favorite of naan but instead of buying store bought chicken and preparing the food ahead of time we want to have a cooking demonstration using wild hog harvested from our neighborhood. So many people won’t eat wild meat because they think it doesn’t taste good. At the Dhaba we want to share the art of cooking wild game. There is a stigma against eating wild hog for many reasons. In the cooking demonstration we want to dispel some of the myths and share food stories about why we harvest wild hogs. The line for Dhaba Tacos can be intimidating so anyone who attends the Hog Wild will receive a line jumper ticket. This is a way we can encourage a different group of participants to interact with our art. We strive to create opportunities for those wishing to engage in art but are not planners or don’t have the ability to bring art themselves. Having our food art be broken into two parts increases the opportunities for others to participate. This year we have a goal of feeding 80 to 100 people. We will have vegan and gluten free options too.
