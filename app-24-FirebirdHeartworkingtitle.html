24 - Firebird Heart (working title)

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

The birth of the heart occurs soon after conception. In November 2024, from a single abstract idea sketch that came from a moment of connection with creative spirit, we iterated a tangible vision and submitted our 2025 Honoraria Letter of Intent to BRC for Firebird (working title). What we are asking for in this Flipside grant proposal is to birth the heart of Firebird and let Flipizens be the nascent beings that not merely witness but also participate in the creation of the spirit of the heart of the larger installation – the pulsing heart of Firebird itself. Different than the Phoenix, this large-scale art installation is our conceptualized version of engaging with the folklore of the Firebird: a legendary bird made of flames that not only symbolizes the transformative experience of voluntary destruction and wisdom rebirth, but also an introspective journey into the generous heart. In Firebird folklore, you and your tribe – your camp – were only given the gift of fire if Firebird determined your heart was sincerely kind. As we build Firebird in DFW for playa placement, we are reaching out to all of our community – not just burners, but friends, collaborators, artists, engineers – anyone with a passion for and dedication to creating meaningful art – to create Firebird. Energy that creates life can be found in a village working together to make beautiful things. Firebird overall will be created using metal, wood, textile, and light, and is estimated in a 25’x25’ space with the bird and wings being approximately 15’ in height. This full installation will be what is placed on playa. However, we are building the nest, bird body, head, articulating wings, tail, and heart separately to then be assembled on playa. The heart will be the nucleus of the installation measuring approximately 2’x2’x3’. The heart will be made from recycled and reused metal and mirror materials. We are aiming for 50% or more prefabricated materials to make it as much of a creative reuse artwork as possible to lessen the impact to the environment and make a statement about how we view consumerism and our waste. The mirror material will reflect whatever it is that Flipizens see within themselves. It will stand on a metal frame supported from underneath. At the center of the heart will be a light source that will be powered by 1-4 hand crank pulley systems that work as a generator to illuminate the heart by the effort of all the Flipizens that choose to try to crank it as bright as it can go. Basically, a Flipizen can intentionally interact with the piece by choosing to crank the generator, thus transforming physical effort into heart light. There are more than one so that Flipizens can crank together, creating light together, if they wish. These cranks will be on stationary pillars placed in an arc approximately 3-5’ around the heart. At least one hand crank will be lower to the ground to allow for those with accessibility considerations and/or children/youth to also interact with the art. A rotary counter will be attached to each hand crank that allows for allocating how many rotations of effort Flipizens have invested to power the heart’s light. This will be creatively integrated into the overall Firebird piece on playa as a recognition of the gift of Flipizens to BRC citizens. When not being cranked and/or at night, a small solar kit will be utilized to keep the heart internally bright. At the end of Flipside, the heart will be removed in its entirety (no burn), the hand cranks will be removed (materials will be reused and the power for the heart will be provided by the large scale installation power supply), the counters tallied, and it will be integrated into the larger Firebird piece as the build completes for on playa assembly. Flipizens would be the only village to have “charged” the heart with powerful fire light. Interacting with the hand cranks to create heart light gives rise to all Flipizens becoming a collaborating artist on the artwork and hopefully creates good vibes that will continue on to playa for BRC participants to experience at Burning Man 2025, giving Firebird a heart birth that was solely created Deep in the Art of Texas.

What is your timeline detailing the planning, creation, installment, and leave no trace removal of your project?

We are currently in pre-production planning for the overall Firebird project and plan on beginning its build as soon as mid-March. Firebird Heart has been conceptualized and is on an accelerated timeline mostly due to the opportunity for this grant funding. It is our plan to source prefabricated materials and complete the final design concept in mid-February 2025. Once we know if our grant proposal was accepted and assuming funding will be provided, we will begin executing our build for Firebird Heart mid-February, which will go through mid-April 2025. We will execute all testing of the power and light interactivity during build prior to mid-April 2025. Installment of the artwork will be based on Flipside recommendations so that it is fully installed, tested, and ready to go prior to the opening day of the event. After the event, the entire artwork will be fully removed from Flipside and proper LNT practices will be executed upon to ensure that the land and environment is returned to the state it was prior to the event. We are dedicated not only to eco-conscious practices throughout build and install, but all activities of the LNT principle to respect the environment and atmosphere of the event overall.
