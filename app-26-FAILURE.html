26 - FAILURE

Description for the Jury: Explain the Project in Full. [This is the description that should explain why the jury should give you the funds so try to answer any questions they might have. Please include some language on how the Flipizens will interact with the piece.]

"Failure" is a large-scale sculptural project constructed entirely from discarded materials sourced through dumpster diving (and other forms of waste/ “trash” collection), second hand stores, and the repurposing of my own "failed" art projects. Accompanied by a looping silent, short film projected onto a screen within the sculpture. This short film will contain footage of the creation process, open source footage of environmentally devastated areas, and theme-relevant poetry and other short form writings placed over portions of the video. This work explores themes such as waste, consumption, and the inherent beauty found in the discarded and overlooked. Our culture generates an overwhelming amount of waste. "Failure" confronts this reality head-on by transforming discarded objects—items deemed no longer “useful or desirable”—into a compelling artistic statement. By giving these materials a new life, the sculpture challenges conventional notions of value and prompts viewers to reconsider their relationship with consumption and disposability. Hopefully, inspiring attempts to discover the beauty or another purpose for our belongings, before discarding them. Exploring the hidden narratives embedded within discarded objects and using art to raise awareness about environmental issues. "Failure" represents a culmination of these interests, offering a powerful visual statement about the consequences of our consumerist culture and the potential for transformation and rebirth. The project incorporates a variety of materials, ranging from discarded plastics and metals to textiles, wood scraps, and components of unfinished or abandoned artwork. These diverse elements will be combined and reconfigured through processes of assemblage, welding, fastening, and layering with any newfound skill I acquire to create this sculpture. If any sound elements would be incorporated, I envision a white noise machine would be the extent. I am a mixed media artist by trade, so learning new mediums and tooling methods is very familiar to my practice. The resulting sculpture will be a complex and dynamic composition including at least several sections of figure-like representations and mask-presenting faces constructed with and adorned with the other found items. Reflecting the chaotic, yet strangely beautiful nature of waste itself enveloping us as we have become unwillingly built into the existing systems of waste. I aim to build an environment around the figure-like sculpture(s), that conveys that the beings within this sculpture are made of the same stuff as all the world around them. Flipizens will be able to walk through the environment. A key element of "Failure" is the inclusion of my own "failed" art projects. These pieces, which did not achieve their initial intended purpose, are not considered failures within my practice. Instead, they represent an essential stage in my creative process, an accelerated launching point aimed towards new possibilities and a reinvigorated, revised purpose (maybe even a bit closer to the fullest sense of its purpose, that could only come to fruition after a re-orienting failure). By integrating these pieces into the larger sculpture, I aim to highlight the inherent value of experimentation and the potential for beauty to emerge from unexpected sources. This process of re-evaluation and repurposing mirrors the project’s overall message about the cyclical nature of creation and destruction and the beauty of it ALL. Due to the nature of the project, I do not have direct depictions of what the outcome will be, but have attached referenced inspirational artists and other sketched options that I aim for, waste-willing. Another intention of this piece is to continue building upon and morphing the works as time progresses. I anticipate a continuous build and morph to expand upon and refine the strong messages associated with the piece. A “living” sculpture that adapts to the social and environmental concerns of the time spent in creation.
